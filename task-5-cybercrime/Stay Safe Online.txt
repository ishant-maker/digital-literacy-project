🛡️ Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist (For College Students in India)
✅ General Online Safety Tips

🔒 Use strong, unique passwords for every account (mix of letters, numbers, symbols).
🔐 Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on email, social media, and banking apps.
📧 Do not click on suspicious links in emails, SMS, or DMs (even if they look official).
🧠 Verify before trusting — double-check unknown contacts asking for personal info.
📱 Install apps only from trusted sources like Google Play Store or App Store.
🔄 Keep your phone, apps, and antivirus updated to avoid security vulnerabilities.
🌐 Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities like banking or login.
👤 Limit what you share online — avoid posting phone numbers, address, or ID details.
💰 UPI & Financial Safety Tips
🚫 Never share your UPI PIN with anyone — banks will never ask for it.
📲 Do not approve “Collect Requests” from unknown people on UPI apps.
⚠️ Be cautious of fake payment screenshots — always check your bank balance.
📞 Ignore calls claiming to be from bank officials asking for OTP or PIN.
🔗 Do not download apps or click links sent for “KYC update” or “reward claims”.
🚨 What To Do If You’re a Victim
⏱️ Act immediately — time is critical in cyber fraud cases.
🌐 Report online at: https://cybercrime.gov.in
📞 Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930
🧾 Save all evidence: screenshots, transaction IDs, messages, emails.
